Method & reproducibility

How every claim is made

Derived and reproducible

Every quantitative claim in this program is derived and reproducible. Inputs are locked before use (no post-hoc tuning); results are obtained only through transformations the lock permits; and each result must pass a reproducible numerical Gate (PASS / FAIL / INCONCLUSIVE) before it is allowed to stand. The code and data behind each number are deposited, versioned, and SHA-256 checksummed under a DOI — reproducibility is guaranteed by the record, not asserted.

No parameter tuning

No constant is fitted to match an observation. Every number is either forced by stated geometry and elementary integrals (no adjustable coefficient), calibrated against a single declared length anchor, or marked open and not claimed.

Derivation, not numerology

Relations such as m_p/m_e = 6π⁵ or ν_p = 3π⁴ are derivations within a fixed lattice geometry, not after-the-fact number-matching: each follows from stated axioms and counting and is graded by how much is forced versus definitional. By an explicit rule, agreement with a measured value is never used to declare the underlying axioms true; a step that is a definitional identification, not a forced result, is labelled as such.

Data and simulation first

The order of work is evidence, then theory. Claims are read off reproducible computations and real datasets and tagged confirmed, refined, or hypothesis; interpretation beyond what the data and the geometry force is avoided or explicitly flagged. Open problems are gathered, not hidden — and independent external re-execution of the code has not yet been performed, which is stated as a social-process gap, not a content gap.
