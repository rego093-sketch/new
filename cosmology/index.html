From inflow · Cosmology

Gravity, galaxies, and cosmology as vacuum inflow

A simulation-grounded account with a runnable check on every claim.

What this establishes

If matter annihilates vacuum quanta at a fixed rate (ν_p = 3π⁴, imported from the physics volume), the surrounding medium flows inward — and that inflow is gravity. The inverse-square law and the weak equivalence principle follow as theorems; one one-parameter law spans the Solar System (Kepler) and galaxies (flat curves) across ten decades; and the galactic scale is derived, not fitted.

Scope & honesty

Most results are labelled degenerate — they reproduce what Newton and ΛCDM already reproduce, a consistency check, not a claim of superiority. The genuinely distinguishing results are few (a₀ = cH₀/2π; the angular-size minimum at z = e − 1 ≈ 1.72). The foundational constants (the per-nucleon rate 3π⁴) are imported from the physics volume. The single fitted astrophysical quantity is one stellar mass-to-light ratio in the NGC 2403 fit; the sharpest tension — the observed absence of vacuum dispersion for light — is stated plainly as the foremost open problem.

Questions

Is this the same as MOND?

On rotation-curve fits, matched assumptions make the two statistically indistinguishable. The difference is that the acceleration scale a₀ is derived here (a₀ = cH₀/2π), not postulated as a fundamental constant.

Why is “dark matter” dark?

In the deepest deficit the quanta have been annihilated faster than they refill, so the core reaches absolute zero — nothing to emit, and no medium for light to propagate through — while its surrounding density gradient still lenses.

Does it need dark energy?

No. The supernova Hubble diagram is fit by d_L = (c/H₀)(1+z)ln(1+z) with no accelerating term; what ΛCDM attributes to dark energy is here the gentle curvature of (1+z)ln(1+z).
