What this establishes

The question is whether a granular jamming → unjamming → liquefaction → suction → sliding mechanism is physically capable of producing a rapid (kyr-scale) phase of continental break-up. It is presented as physical possibility within a falsifiable framework — not as confirmed history — with the opening of the Atlantic as the test case.

Scope & honesty

This is an independent, heterodox theoretical/computational contribution — “self-consistent and reproducible” is not the same as “externally confirmed.” The load-bearing claims remain HOLD pending external evidence. A chronology firewall makes the claim explicitly chronology-agnostic: the high-precision U–Pb break-up ages are not contested and are deferred to as the decisive external test. Several reported verdicts are deliberately negative or HOLD — these are integrity features, not omissions. Numeric claims are audited by validate_all.py (39/39 internal self-consistency checks).

Questions

Does this dispute the dated ages of continental break-up?

No. The U–Pb break-up ages (~201/134/55 Ma) are conceded at full strength; a chronology firewall keeps the claim chronology-agnostic and routes the timescale question to pre-registered falsifiers.

Is rapid break-up claimed as fact?

No. The work establishes physical possibility within a falsifiable framework; the energy source and the laboratory-to-continental scale-up are carried as honest, quantitatively bounded HOLDs.
