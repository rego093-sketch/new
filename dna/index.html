A deterministic two-layer interpretation of DNA

Position is grammar; quantity is runtime.

Read the full paper →

What this establishes

A genome strongly encodes position and form — which physical phase a region prefers, where its structural elements sit, and how deeply fixed they are — while quantity (how much, how often, how fast, how large) is set largely by the cellular runtime. The two layers are read as the two knobs of a phase-separated condensate, and the account is tested on real genomes with every claim tagged confirmed, refined, or hypothesis.

Scope & honesty

Every claim is tagged confirmed, refined, or hypothesis. The framework is a self-gating interpreter with zero hand-tuned parameters; the order of work is data and simulation first, with interpretation beyond what the data force avoided or flagged. Two items are honestly open and gated on one base-pair-precise nucleosome-dyad map: the ~10-bp positioning periodicity (peak at 10.14 bp but faint, 1.14×) and the A-tract term.

Questions

What does “position is grammar, quantity is runtime” mean?

The sequence fixes where structural elements sit and what shape a region takes; how much of any output is produced is set largely by the cellular runtime, not by the letters.

Is body size written in the genome?

No — and this is where it is hardest to fake. Sequence and protein form are conserved across up to ~1,200,000× body mass with no size signal; size is the runtime integral of conserved readers acting over time.
