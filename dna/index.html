A deterministic two-layer interpretation of DNA

Position is grammar; quantity is runtime.

Read the full paper →

What this establishes

A genome strongly encodes position and form — which physical phase a region prefers, where its structural elements sit, and how deeply fixed they are — while quantity (how much, how often, how fast, how large) is set by a few named genomic programs acting through the cellular runtime. The two layers are read as the two knobs of a phase-separated condensate; form and size turn out to be two readings of a single developmental switch — its discrete state and its graded dwell. Every claim is tested on real genomes and tagged confirmed, refined, or hypothesis, with refuted leads kept visible in an open failure log.

Featured derivations

Two layers, two knobs
form ← γ (Layer 1, sequence) ; quantity ← φ (Layer 2, runtime)

Structures are read as phase-separated condensates: composition sets the interfacial-tension propensity γ; the runtime sets the fill fraction φ and supplies materials.

Form and size, one switch
state ⇒ form (on/off) ; dwell ∝ γ^(3/2) ⇒ size

One bistable developmental switch is read two ways: its discrete ON/OFF state makes a structure present or absent (clean losses — a snake's limb, a human's tail — are a retained circuit held below threshold), while how long it dwells ON sets cumulative cell number, i.e. size.

Size's magnitude — named, not “runtime”
size ≈ cell# = (proliferation) × (open duration)

The switch sets the direction and duration of growth; the absolute magnitude is not handed to “the runtime” but factorised over named genomic programs — the let-7/HMGA2/LIN28B timing clock, GH/IGF1 dosage, and a polygenic background (human height: ~12,111 variants concentrated in growth-plate genes).

Scope & honesty

Every claim is tagged confirmed, refined, or hypothesis, and the order of work is data and simulation first, with interpretation beyond what the data force avoided or flagged. Refuted, withdrawn, or inadmissible leads are not quietly deleted but relocated to an open failure log — for example, an earlier cross-family “body-size law” (a pooled mouse/cat/tiger/elephant correlation, r = +0.70) is withdrawn because an independent five-primate order inverts it (r = −0.47); what survives is the narrower, stronger within-lineage PTHLH result. The framework still carries zero hand-tuned parameters. Two fine-scale items remain honestly open and gated on one base-pair-precise nucleosome-dyad map — the ~10-bp positioning periodicity (peak at 10.14 bp but faint, 1.14×) and the A-tract term — and the absolute magnitude of size is an explicitly open, factorised target.

Questions

What does “position is grammar, quantity is runtime” mean?

The sequence fixes where structural elements sit and what shape a region takes; how much of any output is produced is set by a few named genomic programs acting through the cellular runtime, not by the letters in isolation.

Is body size written in the genome?

Its direction and duration are: a single switch-element's stiffness sets how long a growth program stays open, and dwell time × proliferation gives size. The absolute magnitude is not handed to “the runtime” but factorised over named genomic programs — the let-7/HMGA2/LIN28B timing clock, GH/IGF1 dosage, and a polygenic background — acting over developmental time. Sequence and protein form stay conserved across up to ~1,200,000× body mass with no size signal.

How can one switch encode both form and size?

It is read two ways. The switch's discrete state — ON or OFF — decides whether a structure is built at all (an all-or-nothing readout that explains within-species fixedness and clean losses, like a snake's limb or a human's tail). How long it dwells in the ON state — a graded quantity scaling as γ^(3/2) — sets how much is built. Form is the state; size is the dwell.
