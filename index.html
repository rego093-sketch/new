Overview

This project publishes a long‑form whitepaper (hundreds of pages) and a validated reproducibility bundle. The goal of this landing page is not to duplicate the full text, but to provide a machine‑readable index: what the document contains, how it is structured, and how results are reproduced via DOI‑anchored artifacts.

Core statement: VP models vacuum/space as a jammed lattice of discrete volume units. Observed “softness” or “fluidity” is treated as a rate of structural failure (unjamming events) rather than an intrinsic softness of the underlying units.

What is inside the whitepaper

  • Axioms & definitions: notation, locked inputs, and the meaning of “jamming/rigidity”.
  • Derivations: geometry‑driven derivations under the one‑way rule (LOCK→Derive→Gate).

  • Fluidity as failure rate (φ): a protocol‑locked definition based on unjamming event counting.

  • Acoustic calibration (Appendix K): classical isomorphism used as a calibration test (not an analogy).

Appendices (why they matter)

Appendices are used to separate interpretation and validation from locked derivations. This prevents post‑hoc tuning: the main chain stays LOCK→Derive→Gate, while appendices provide external checks and application‑layer demonstrations.

What “LOCK→Derive→Gate” means

LOCK freezes inputs/definitions/protocols. Derive allows only declared transformations. Gate outputs PASS/FAIL/INCONCLUSIVE with evidence pointers.
